## What Does My D&D Character Sheets (DDCS) App Do?

While there's no perfect solution out there that works for everyone, I think this table below helps make it clear how my D&D Character Sheets compares to other options. 

### Comparison Table of DDCS and Other Options
| Feature / Benefit | DnDBeyond, Roll20, etc | Pencil & Paper | Fillable PDF | Spreadsheet | DDCS |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Totally free | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Familiar / official layout | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Comes with pre-populated content | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Supports homebrew & custom content | ⚠️ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |

| Easy spell list management | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Works the same on any computer/OS | ✅ | ❌ | ⚠️ | ⚠️ | ✅ |
| No/minimal risk of losing / damaging your sheet | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Flexible text formatting & layout | ❌ | ⚠️ | ⚠️ | ⚠️ | ✅ |

The main thing you'll quickly notice is that, unlike most other apps out there, I have intentionally not included autocalculations (like one-click Long Rests) or prepopulated SRD content. 

The main reason for this is I want to keep this as flexible (and barrier-free) as possible for homebrew, and not get close to any legal / licensed content messes.

This is meant a highly flexible BYO-content digital character sheet that sticks fairly closely to the general layout we're familiar with from the official downloadable ones (5E and 5.5E). A way to make unlimited player characters and use all the home-brew you want—for free! 🙂

## Features Currently Available
- Free to use, no subscription required
- Familiar layout based on the official D&D character sheet (5E and 5.5E compatible)
- Organized storage so you never lose a sheet
- Unlimited characters (add, duplicate, delete as you like)
- Supports any 5E or 5.5E compatible content — homebrew, house rules, non-standard builds
- Resizable & rich text formatting for bigger text blocks
- A sortable and expandable table for spells
- Pop-out spell detail cards that can be resized, moved around, and formatted 
- Fully searchable interface (use Command + F and find that buried feat)
- Light and dark mode
- Consistent display across operating systems (currently only desktop supported, but I'm working on that)
- No app install needed
- Export to PDF (basic—retains formatting but uses quite a few pages)

## Features I Hope to Implement
- Tablet and mobile compatibility
- Content imports (you're responsible to own it)
- Minimal formulas (i.e. When you change your Ability Score, automatically adjusting your modifier)
- Better options for class-specific content (i.e. Druid Wild Shapes)
- A way to visually differentiate one character sheet from another (i.e. probably colour scheme; probably not handling image uploads)

## Features DDCS Won't Be Offering
- Automated math (to keep total homebrew/content flexibility, almost no fields are locked into a specific function, so you update it manually, like you would with pencil and paper)
- Pre-populated content (you are responsible to source and add content you own/have permission to use)
- Fancy animations (I want to keep this app light and quick)
- Other TTRPG systems or versions (this is just for D&D 5.5E and 5E)
